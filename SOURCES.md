## Note on Sources

This documentation draws from:
- **Traditional Scottish and English folklore** — Collected by 18th-19th century folklorists
- **Regional oral traditions** — Border country, Highlands, Northern England
- **Academic folklore studies** — Scholarly analysis of household spirit traditions
- **Historical documents** — Kirk records, local histories, early modern texts
- **Comparative studies** — Cross-cultural household spirit research

The Brownie represents:
- Pre-Christian household spirit beliefs
- Rural agricultural culture
- Pre-industrial domestic life
- Scottish and Northern English cultural heritage

The tradition is particularly well-documented due to extensive 19th-century folklore collection efforts in Scotland and England during the Romantic period.
